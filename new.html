A Closer Look At Cancer

Cancer is a disease characterized by the uncontrolled growth of abnormal cells, which can invade and damage surrounding tissues and spread to other parts of the body. As a leading cause of death worldwide, millions of people succumb to cancer each year. This visualization explores the prevalence of cancer across different countries and regions, providing insights into mortality rates and the impact of the disease on various populations. Additionally, it examines cancer death data in Malaysia, focusing on age groups, cancer types, and sex, to gain a better understanding of the global burden of this disease and the regions most affected by it.

Cancer Deaths Worldwide: A Yearly Overview

Notably in 2021, Japan recorded the highest cancer death rate in Asia, with an alarming 35.25 deaths per 10,000 population. In contrast, Malaysia reported a significantly lower cancer death rate of 8.98 deaths per 10,000 population. This visualization effectively highlights the disparities in cancer mortality rates, shedding light on the varying impact of cancer across different regions.

Worldwide Cancer Death Rates: Regional and Population Insights

The size of each circle represents the population of the respective country, with larger circles indicating higher populations.

Trends in Cancer Death Rates by Age Group in Malaysia (2000-2021)

It is remarkable to observe a significant trend in cancer mortality, particularly as individuals reach the age of 50, where the death rate begins to increase markedly. This trend becomes even more pronounced for those aged 50 and above, with rates escalating significantly as age progresses into the 85+ category. Notably, the highest cancer death rate is observed in 2018 for the age group of 85 and older, highlighting the critical impact of age on cancer mortality rates.

Cancer Mortality in Malaysia: A Comparative Analysis by Type (2000-2021)

The data reveals that the first five highest cancer mortality rates in Malaysia are associated with mouth and oropharynx cancers, liver cancer, breast cancer, colon and rectum cancers, and trachea, bronchus, and lung cancers. Notably, trachea, bronchus, and lung cancers consistently recorded the highest death rates among these types, maintaining this position throughout the analyzed years. This trend underscores the persistent impact of lung-related cancers on mortality rates in Malaysia, reflecting ongoing public health challenges and the pressing need for continued awareness and intervention strategies to address these critical health issues.

Cancer Death Rates in Malaysia: A Gender Perspective (2000-2021)

Both genders experienced a peak in cancer mortality rates in 2018, with males reaching 61.10 deaths per 100,000 people and females recording 54.78 deaths per 100,000 people. Notably, there was a rapid decline in cancer death rates for both genders in 2019 and 2021, indicating a significant reduction in mortality associated with cancer during these years.
