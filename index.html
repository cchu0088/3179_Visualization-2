A Closer Look At Cancer

Cancer is a leading cause of death worldwide, with millions of people succumbing to the disease each year. This visualization explores the prevalence of cancer across different countries, providing insights into the mortality rates and the impact of the disease on various populations. By examining the data on cancer deaths, we can gain a better understanding of the global burden of this disease and the regions most affected by it.

Global Cancer Mortality Rates

Notably, Japan recorded the highest cancer death rate in Asia, with an alarming 35.25 deaths per 10,000 population. In contrast, Malaysia reported a significantly lower cancer death rate of 8.98 deaths per 10,000 population. This visualization effectively highlights the disparities in cancer mortality rates, shedding light on the varying impact of cancer across different regions.
