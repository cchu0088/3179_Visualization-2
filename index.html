Cancer Mortality: Global Overview and Focus on Malaysia (2000-2020)

This page demonstrates how you can use pure.css grid for your layout.

The choropleth map visualizes global cancer deaths from malignant neoplasms for the years 2000 to 2021. Each country is colored based on the number of cancer deaths per 10,000 population, allowing for an easy comparison of cancer mortality rates across different regions. The interactive year selection feature enables users to explore trends over time, highlighting variations in cancer death rates worldwide.

Diverging Stacked Bar Chart

The diverging stacked bar chart displays age-standardized deaths from malignant neoplasms in males and females per 100,000 people for the year 2021. It provides a clear visual distinction between male and female cancer death rates, with bars extending in opposite directions from a central axis. This allows for a direct comparison of cancer mortality between genders and highlights disparities in death rates across different countries.

The line chart illustrates cancer death rates in Malaysia by age group from 2000 to 2021. Each line represents a different age group, enabling viewers to track changes in cancer mortality over time. The interactive legend allows users to highlight specific age groups, facilitating a deeper understanding of how cancer death rates have evolved across different demographics in Malaysia over the years.
